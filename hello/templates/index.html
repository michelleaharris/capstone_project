Thank you for visiting my website! I'm Michelle Harris and this is my submission for the Data Incubator Challenge. You may have noticed by the title that it is Chicago themed. I analyzed and compared two Chicago databases: one regarding energy usage and one regarding census information for neighborhoods. Check out Round 3 for further analysis. The first chart is a simple overview to begin.

The plot below simply shows the total energy usage in kiloWatt hours for different neighborhoods in Chicago. As one would expect, the neighborhoods with by far the greatest energy usage tend to be in the downtown area: the Loop, Near North Side, etc.

In these scatter plots, I am looking to assess the extent the local salary has an effect on the energy usage in the neighborhood. The first scatter plot compares the per capita income of each neighborhood to the total energy usage per square foot of each building. The second scatter plot compares the per capita income of each neighborhood to the total energy usage per person in only residential buildings. I expect there to be a greater correlation in the plot than the first. The first plot includes commercial buildings which make up the majority of the downtown area. Though, there seems to be little to no correlation for energy use in residential buildings to per capita income. For the second plot, a simple linear regression returns a slope of -14 and an R2 value of 0.000289. For the first plot, the slope is - 0.03 with an R2 value of 0.006241. See further thoughts and conclusions below the plots.

Preliminary Conclusions

In general, both are poorly correlated, but surprisingly income is more positively correlated with energy usage per square footage in commercial and residential buildings. This could be due to two scenarios. The first being that most residential buildings in a large city also tend to have commercial enterprises as well though they are primarily residential. The second scenario that would lead to such high energy usage per person in residential buildings could also be increased general usage of electricity by the building in common areas. The most likley case is the first one, but a more detailed description of each building would be required.
